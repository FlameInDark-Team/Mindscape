Mindscape v1 · Scientific Basis

What is evidence, and what is just a product choice

The previous version of this document overstated several claims. This revision keeps the product grounded in what is supported by public research and clearly labels where Mindscape is making a pragmatic v1 design choice.

Source What it supports Mindscape implication
WHO Mental-health conditions often begin before age 24, so higher education is a critical early-intervention setting. Colleges are a valid first market and intervention context.
Healthy Minds Network College mental-health prevalence, help-seeking, and campus-level variation can be benchmarked at national scale. Mindscape should expose cohort-level prevalence and participation patterns, not only individual anecdotes.
Help-seeking and stigma study Stigma and social concerns reduce treatment-seeking among college students. The student flow must be low-stigma, short, and anonymous by default.
PHQ-4 study in college students Ultra-brief anxiety/depression screening is usable in campus populations. Weekly PHQ-2 + GAD-2 is a defensible v1 choice.
GAD-2 review GAD-2 has acceptable to good screening accuracy. Weekly anxiety screening can stay short and still be useful.
AUCCCD survey Counseling support is linked to academic and persistence benefits. The buyer value proposition should emphasize retention and support operations, not only wellness language.
Passive sensing review Passive sensing is promising but methodologically uneven and privacy-sensitive. Passive sensing stays out of the core v1 risk engine.

Keep

  • Fact Short validated screening is better than ad-hoc scoring language.
    Mindscape should not claim that a mood slider is equivalent to PHQ-9.
  • Fact Frequent self-report helps reduce recall distortion.
    That supports daily pulse design, but not a claim that daily pulse alone can diagnose risk.
  • Fact Help-seeking friction matters.
    Anonymous first-touch design is a product response to that evidence.

Rewrite

  • Product choice “Trend slope matters.”
    Keep this as a product rule, not a scientific claim that trend is universally more predictive than current state.
  • Product choice “Late-night check-ins add risk.”
    Treat as a soft operational observation only, or leave it out of the core risk logic entirely.
  • Product choice “AI text severity changes risk.”
    In v1, AI should tag themes and suggest resources; it should not be the primary risk engine.

Explicit non-claims

  • Mindscape is not a diagnosis system.
    It is an early-support and routing product.
  • Mindscape does not identify anonymous students behind the scenes.
    Identity only appears when a student explicitly requests follow-up.
  • Mindscape does not claim clinically validated passive sensing in v1.
    That work is deferred to a later ethics-reviewed pilot.
